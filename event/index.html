Who we are

Love Is Not Tourism is a global grassroots movement dedicated to reuniting the binational couples and families who have been forcibly separated, indefinitely, by travel bans, and border closures during the COVID-19 crisis.

Our Solution

Implement simple humanitarian exemptions to travel bans that follow safety protocols like quarantining and robust testing (we will even pay for it!), just like in Denmark, Germany, Norway, Switzerland, Netherlands, and others!

Educate, Support, and Amplify!

On December 26th, 2020, we invite all to a day of advocacy to learn about, support, and amplify Love Is Not Tourism, a global grassroots movement advocating for reasonable and safe humanitarian exemptions to international travel bans for separated binational partners and families.

For months, couples have been prevented from implementing their life plans together, including reuniting, moving in together, getting married, and being present at the birth of their child because of a lack of equal protections under the law for non-married couples. As a result, they have been waiting in desperation, anxiety, and frustration for nearly a year to see their partners and families again with no clear end date in sight. Together, we will honor and support the thousands of binational couples and families across the world who continue to be separated, indefinitely, by amplifying our calls to governments worldwide.

How to Participate

The easiest way is to post a photo of someone you love and use the hashtag #LoveIsNotTourism on any social media platform to call on governments to safely reunite loved ones through compassionate exemptions that make use of safety protocols like quarantining and robust testing to keep us all safe while we reunite. You can also share stories, artwork, and videos–just do not forget the hashtag and try tagging your politicians too!
